Shipping to South Africa

Every year King & Wilson helps hundreds of clients relocate from Australia to South Africa.

We have the people, processes and infrastructure to make your move stress-free. Here's how it works:

  • We conduct an in-home consultation to guide you through the moving process, recommend a container size and determine the best shipping schedule for you. This process usually takes between 30 minutes and one hour.
  • On the scheduled day, we visit your home to pack your cartons and wrap your furniture to export standards.
  • Your belongings are packed into a shipping line container, transferred to our warehouse and then onto the appropriate ship.
  • We ensure all of your necessary paperwork is in order to facilitate prompt customs clearance.
  • We deliver the belongings to your new home in South Africa - unpacking the cartons, placing furniture and disposing of any mess.

We pride ourselves on providing you with as much assistance as you need to make your move stress free. Should you require it, we can arrange:

  • Storage of your belongings in Australia or South Africa
  • Cleaning of your Australian home
  • Visas and foreign exchange

Frequently asked questions

I only want to ship a few items. Can you still help me?

Yes we can. Our excess baggage department specialises in packing and shipping small quantities of unaccompanied personal effects by air.

To get an instant baggage quote, please click here.

Can I pack my boxes myself?

Yes, you can pack your own boxes. Some clients elect to pack clothes, books and linen and leave the more fragile items to us. If you choose to do this, we will supply you with appropriate export quality boxes and packing materials.

We can also provide you with instructions for packing your boxes to export standards, however we can not be responsible for any breakages in boxes packed by you.

How long will it take for my belongings to arrive?

Shipping from Australia to South Africa takes 6-8 weeks depending on the final destination in South Africa.

If you require some of your items to arrive within a shorter timeframe, we recommend utilising our air freight service. Many of our clients find this to be a more cost-effective solution than paying airline excess baggage.

Do you deliver to a house or apartment?

Our standard international moving service includes delivery to a residence up to first floor with clear vehicle access from the street. This service is inclusive of checking off the packing list, placing furniture and boxes into nominated rooms, unwrapping and re-assembling furniture items, unpacking boxes onto flat surfaces and removing the packing debris and rubbish.

Our international baggage services are priced to be a more economical transport solution and therefore delivery is to ground floor only and excludes unpacking.

Can I track my shipment?

Yes, we provide our customers with regular email updates and a client login to our website which allows you to monitor the progress of your shipment.

Is there anything that I can't ship?

For safety and security there are some items that cannot be shipped. Most of these items fall under the classification of prohibited belongings such as LPG gas bottles, paints, aerosols and solvents.

Download a full list of prohibited items here.

Do I need insurance?

Whilst it is unusual for items to be damaged or lost in transit, for your peace of mind we recommend that you take out transit insurance. To help you get the right cover, we have put together a cost-effective policy specifically designed to protect your belongings during transit and/or storage in our warehouses.

Request a quote or contact us to speak to a moving consultant about the appropriate insurance for your move.

Will I have to pay at South African customs?

The importation of used personal effects into South Africa is duty and tax free provided the person importing their belongings has been living outside South Africa longer than 12 months and the goods have been owned and used for longer than 12 months.

The exemptions are alcohol and tobacco which are both taxable.

For more information on South Africa's customs regulations, please download our guide.
